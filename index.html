Hyderabad


Hyderabad is a city of Nawabs. The people of Hyderabad is called Hyderabadis. Hyderabad was founded in 1591 AD by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah. Hyderabad is the capital of southern India's Telangana state. A major center for the technology industry, it's home to many upscale restaurants and shops. Its historic sites include Golconda Fort, a former diamond-trading center that was once the Qutb Shahi dynastic capital. The Charminar, a 16th-century mosque whose 4 arches support towering minarets, is an old city landmark near the long-standing Laad Bazaar. Area: 650 km² Elevation: 542 m
